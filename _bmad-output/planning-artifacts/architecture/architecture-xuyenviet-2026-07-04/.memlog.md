- (decision) Knowledge card is the canonical aggregate only after deterministic validation and independent AI judging. Extracted candidate claims remain immutable job/evaluation artifacts; no separate claim aggregate exists in the MVP. Evidence, evaluations, relations, and review recommendations depend on the card.
- (decision) Knowledge state mutations use PostgreSQL as source of truth with a transactional outbox/dirty index marker. Knowledge owns all state transitions; each transition updates card state, audit, and index state atomically. Retrieval fail-closed re-checks current owner-row eligibility before source-bundle use. High-risk conflict/source removal immediately downgrades or suppresses and disables the projection; indexing is asynchronous, idempotent by card ID and content version.
- (decision) Knowledge audit is intentionally lean at scale: knowledge cards retain current state, monotonic content version, current judge summary, and currently effective evidence only. Durable audit records only meaningful state transitions and operator actions. Extraction/evaluation artifacts are operational data with short retention, not an immutable fact-version graph.
- (decision) Evidence is retained separately from active use. New evidence supplements current evidence by default; it replaces/deactivates older evidence only for time-varying facts or when newer evidence makes old evidence unsuitable. Retrieval uses only active evidence, capped at three supporting and one conflicting record per card, selected by recency, source independence, and quality. Inactive evidence has short operational retention with a deactivation reason.
- (decision) Candidate relation boundary: scope candidate matching by knowledge type plus normalized location/route; only auto-attach when code validates scope/evidence and an independent judge confirms same fact with equivalent conditions. Create a new card for materially distinct compatible facts; suppress redundant/same-source candidates; recommend review for ambiguous relations, state-changing merge, conflict, high-risk topic, or missing observed date.
- (decision) Ingestion uses one stateful orchestration job per source capture version with stages queued, triaging, extracting, judging, relating, and terminal published/suppressed/review-recommended/verify-first/failed outcomes. A failed stage retries without re-running completed stages. Indexing remains a separate projection worker because it also responds to operator edits, source removal, and conflict transitions.
- (decision) Verification is a separate dimension, not a publication state: verification_state is not_required, required, corroborated, or failed. Required claims may be active only as conditional caveats and cannot drive itinerary decisions; corroboration does not automatically imply confirmed; failed verification suppresses or downgrades by risk.
- (decision) Automation uses a dedicated service actor, system-knowledge-pipeline. Source submitter remains provenance only; automated triage, judge, relation, publication, conflict downgrade, and indexing mutations are not attributed to the submitter. Audits link ingestion job, source, model/prompt version, and submitter where applicable.
